FOREWORD

Born in the U.S., based in Virginia with Vietnamese and Black roots, I build scenes that feel like pages you want to keep. I don’t promise to change your life. I show up real and let the work talk.

Free speech isn’t decoration — it’s the air a performer breathes. My site treats it like a stage: messy, loud, hand-drawn, honest. If it looks like someone wrote over their yearbook, that’s the point.

Acting, singing, and dance are the language. Everything else is texture: grain, ink, torn paper, tape shadows. The goal? A place that feels lived-in — like a creative journal you’re allowed to open.

Read what matters, skip what doesn’t, and if you disagree — say it. I’d rather make something that asks for a response than something that asks for permission.
